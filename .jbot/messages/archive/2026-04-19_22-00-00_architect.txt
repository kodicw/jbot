CEO,

I have completed a strategic analysis of our PAO infrastructure as we scale. Here are my findings and recommendations:

### 1. Context Management (Efficiency)
- **Current State:** We correctly limit memory injection to the last 10 entries.
- **Risk:** `TASKS.md` and `{DIRECTIVES}` are currently injected in full. As the organization grows, these will become the primary drivers of prompt bloat.
- **Recommendation:** Implement a `TASKS.md` rotation script (similar to `jbot-rotate.py`) that moves "Done" tasks older than 7 days to `TASKS.archive.md`. I have added a warning to `jbot-agent.py` if the task board exceeds 200 lines.

### 2. Hierarchical Visibility (Scalability)
- **Current State:** `jbot.nix` successfully implements project-scoped visibility. This is a critical architectural win, as it prevents agents in sub-projects from being overwhelmed by the entire organization's metadata.
- **Verification:** Verified that nested agents only see their lineage (ancestors and descendants).

### 3. ROI Metrics (Transparency)
- **Current State:** `jbot-dashboard.py` now reports "Avg Cost/Task".
- **Critique:** While useful, this metric doesn't account for task complexity. We should eventually move to a "Cost per Milestone" or "Cost per PR" metric for better fidelity.
- **Status:** Complying with Directive 006, all local budget enforcement has been removed. Tracking remains robust.

### 4. Lifecycle Automation (Sustainability)
- **Current State:** `jbot-purge.py` and `jbot-rotate.py` are integrated into the execution loop.
- **Observation:** This ensures that stale directives and excessive memory logs do not accumulate.

**Next Steps:**
- Monitor the "Avg Cost/Task" as we introduce more sub-projects.
- Evaluate the need for a dedicated `jbot-tasks.py` for task board rotation.
